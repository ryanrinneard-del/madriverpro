The Short Game · Clinic

Get the ball close from around the green.

The short game is where scores are made — more than half your shots happen inside 40 yards. Here is our hour together in one place: one reliable Foundation Shot, and the small, sensible tweaks that fit whatever the course gives you.

Print the One-Page Plan

Master one shot first. Everything else is a small tweak on top of it.

The Whole Idea
Section 1 · Long Putts

Roll it close.

Stop trying to make it. Cozy the ball into a three-foot circle so you’re left with a tap-in — because almost every three-putt comes from poor speed, not a poor read.

01 · Speed

Distance is king.

Rehearse looking at the hole, not the ball — your body sizes the stroke to the distance. Match backstroke and through-stroke on the same unhurried tempo, and die it into the circle.

02 · Grip

Quiet the hands.

Palms facing each other, thumbs down the flat top of the grip, a light reverse-overlap so the hands work as one. There’s no single “right” grip — pick the one you can repeat.

03 · Touch

Soft pressure — 4 / 10.

Light hands feel the weight of the head; a tight grip kills your touch. From off the green, a so-so putt usually beats a so-so chip — don’t fear the Texas wedge.

Section 2 · Chipping

The Foundation Shot.

The scoring zone

The chip is the shot everything else is built on — low, running, and boringly repeatable. Three keys: let the loft lift it, hit slightly down (ball first, then grass), and swing straight back and through with quiet hands.

Drill

Ball First, Then Turf

The fastest way to feel a clean, downward strike.

  1. Lay a tee or a chalk line just in front of the ball, on the target side.
  2. Chip so you brush the grass in front of the ball — never behind it.
  3. When your divots start after the ball, you’ve found it.
Set-up: sole flat, chest over the ball, about 60% on your lead foot and stay there, hands a touch ahead.
Section 3 · Pitching

Same shot, bigger swing.

A pitch is your chip’s taller sibling — higher and longer, but the exact same Foundation Shot. The engine gets bigger; the fundamentals don’t change. Watch four things (we used Rory McIlroy, front-on).

01 · Hips

Lead hip drives forward.

The lead hip starts over the ball and moves toward the target through the shot. Your weight goes forward — you don’t hang back.

02 · Chest

Stay tall & level.

The chest stays vertical, no lean to the side. Rotate around a steady, upright body — don’t tip or sway.

03 · Wrist

Keep the trail-wrist bend.

Coming down, the trail wrist stays bent back — that holds the loft and delivers a crisp, downward strike instead of a scoop.

04 · Release

Split the triangle.

Just after impact the shaft points down the middle of the triangle your arms and shoulders make — proof the body led and the hands stayed quiet.
