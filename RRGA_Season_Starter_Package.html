Adult Assessment — 90 Minutes
Follow this sequence for every adult Season Starter intake.
Minutes 0–10 · Welcome & Goal Setting

The Conversation First

Before any club is swung. This is the most important part of the session.

  • Ask: What brought you in? What have you tried before?
  • Ask: What does success look like for you this season?
  • Note injury history, physical limitations, time available to practice
  • Ask: What's the one thing that's costing you shots right now?
LISTEN DON'T ADVISE YET
Minutes 10–20 · TPI Movement Screen

Physical Baseline

Quick G3 screen — identify limitations before prescribing swing changes.

  • Overhead deep squat, toe touch, single-leg balance
  • Hip rotation (seated + standing), shoulder mobility
  • Note any red flags that affect swing capacity
  • This informs what you prescribe — and what you don't
TPI G3
Minutes 20–40 · Ball Flight Baseline

TrackMan Diagnostic

Let the data speak. Don't coach yet — just observe and collect.

  • 5 drives — observe pattern, note face-to-path relationship
  • 5 mid-irons (7i) — check consistency, contact quality
  • 5 wedge shots (PW, 50 yards) — observe technique under low-pressure conditions
  • Record: carry, ball speed, spin, launch angle, face angle, club path
  • Video: face-on and down-the-line of 2–3 swings each
TRACKMAN VIDEO NO COACHING YET
Minutes 40–55 · Short Game & Putting

Scoring Zone Assessment

Where most shots are won or lost. Don't skip this.

  • 5 chips from tight lie, 10 yards off green — observe contact and trajectory
  • 5 pitch shots, 30–40 yards — observe technique and distance control awareness
  • Putting: 10 putts from 6 feet — observe alignment, face control, routine
  • Ask: How do you feel about your short game? What would you change?
SHORT GAME PUTTING
Minutes 55–75 · Analysis & Prescription

The Debrief

This is where the assessment earns its value. Be clear, be direct, be human.

  • Show 2–3 TrackMan data points that tell the story simply
  • Show the video — use it to confirm the data, not replace it
  • Identify no more than 3 priority areas (resist the urge to fix everything)
  • Explain: "Here's what your ball is doing. Here's why. Here's what we'll do about it."
  • Reference their stated goals from minute 0 — connect findings to outcomes they care about
VISION54 APPROACH CONNECT DATA TO OUTCOMES
Minutes 75–90 · Written Prescription & Next Steps

Leave With a Plan

Every student walks away with something written. No exceptions.

  • Hand them the written prescription (3 priorities, 1–2 drills each)
  • Walk through the 5-lesson arc — what each phase addresses
  • Set expectations: "Here's how we'll know it's working."
  • Book the first lesson before they leave the tee
  • Confirm email address for the welcome email (Acuity triggers Mailchimp)

Junior Assessment — 60 Minutes
Adjusted pace, age-appropriate language. Parents present for first 10 and last 15 minutes.
Minutes 0–10 · Parent + Junior Welcome

Set Expectations With the Family

  • Ask parents: What are your goals for your junior this season?
  • Ask the junior directly: What do you love about golf? What do you find hard?
  • Briefly explain what you'll be doing today — demystify it
  • Parents step back for the session (reduces performance anxiety)
Minutes 10–35 · Ball Flight & Basics

Observe, Don't Judge

  • Free swing — let them show you what they've got, no instruction yet
  • Watch grip, setup, balance, contact quality
  • Use TrackMan if appropriate to age/stage — frame it as a "launch game"
  • Short game: a few chips and putts — watch naturally, ask how they feel
OBSERVE FIRSTKEEP IT FUN
Minutes 35–45 · Quick Wins

One Concept, Real Improvement

  • Pick one thing to address — something that will make the biggest immediate difference
  • Give it, see the improvement, celebrate it genuinely
  • This is what they'll remember — and what their parents will see
ONE CHANGE ONLY
Minutes 45–60 · Parent Debrief + Development Profile

Give Parents a Clear Picture

  • Bring parents back in — talk about their junior in front of the junior
  • Share 2–3 genuine strengths you observed
  • Explain the 5-lesson arc and what each phase will address
  • Hand them the written Player Development Profile
  • Book lesson 1 and confirm email for the welcome sequence
