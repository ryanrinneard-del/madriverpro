# Product Marketing Context — RR Golf at Mad River

*Last updated: 2026-04-04*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Ryan Rinneard is the Director of Instruction at Mad River Golf Club, offering year-round golf coaching, programs, and experiences that help golfers find consistency, confidence, and more fun on the course.
**What it does:** RR Golf at Mad River provides private lessons, lesson packages, on-course instruction, golf schools, junior development, ladies programming, and unique social golf experiences (Thursday Night Experiences) to Mad River members and the surrounding Collingwood/Creemore area. In the off-season, Ryan coaches indoors at Ace Track Golf in Collingwood, ON.
**Product category:** Golf instruction / Golf coaching / Golf experiences
**Product type:** Service-based (in-person instruction + experiential events)
**Business model:** Direct booking via Acuity Scheduling. Private lessons (CA$110–$375), lesson series with volume discounts (5/10/15/20 packs), group clinics (CA$40–$60), experiential events (CA$55–$165), junior camps (CA$595/week), golf schools (CA$199).

## Target Audience
**Target market:** Golfers in the Creemore, Collingwood, Blue Mountains, Wasaga Beach, and Barrie areas of Ontario. Currently skews toward Mad River Golf Club members, with a goal to expand to the broader local community.
**Demographics:** Equal mix of men and women. All ages — adults and juniors. Juniors range from elite competitive players to complete beginners.
**Skill levels:** Full range but skews intermediate+. Beginners typically enter through group clinics and ladies programming.
**Primary use case:** Golfers who want consistency so they stop embarrassing themselves on the course.
**Jobs to be done:**
- "I want to be more consistent so I don't embarrass myself in front of my playing partners"
- "I want to break [100/90/80/70] or lower my handicap"
- "I want to take my game from the range to the course"
- "I want to have more fun playing golf"
- "I want to meet like-minded women who play golf at my level" (Ladies Academy)
- "I want a social golf experience, not just a lesson" (Thursday Night Experiences)
**Use cases:**
- Intermediate golfer frustrated by inconsistency books a lesson series to build a reliable game
- New Mad River member joins Ladies Golf Series to make friends and feel comfortable on the course
- Couple or friend group books a Thursday Night Experience (Wine & Wedges, Scotch & Short Game, 9 & Dine) as a social outing
- Parent enrolls their junior in Train to Play camp or private lessons
- Competitive junior works with Ryan on performance coaching
- Golfer stuck in a YouTube spiral books a private lesson to get a clear, personalized plan

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Golfers can't take their game from the range to the course. They feel inconsistent, frustrated, and embarrassed in front of their peers.
**What they've tried:**
- Watching endless YouTube videos
- Taking tips from friends on the course
- Grinding on the driving range
- Chasing every new tip they hear until their head is "full of garbage" and they don't know what they're doing anymore
**Why those solutions fail:** YouTube doesn't know how *you* move *your* body. Tips from friends are generic and often contradictory. Range grinding without guidance just ingrains bad habits. None of these approaches address the mental/emotional game.
**What it costs them:** Embarrassment on the course, lost enjoyment of the game, stalled improvement despite effort, money spent on equipment that doesn't fix the real issue.
**Emotional tension:** Negative self-talk, zero emotional resilience, fight-or-flight state on the course, high cortisol, panic over shots in front of peers. Many golfers are stuck in a cycle of frustration where the mental game sabotages whatever technical progress they've made.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors:**
- Georgian Bay Club (golf instruction) — standard lesson model, no experiential programming
- Oslerbrook Golf Club (golf instruction) — standard lesson model
- Steve Moore Golf — individual pro, no lifestyle/experience offerings
- The Golf Club, Collingwood — indoor facility, no on-course or experiential programming

**Secondary competitors:** YouTube golf instruction, Golf Digest tips, swing analysis apps — fall short because they don't know the individual's body, movement patterns, or mental game.

**Indirect competitors:** "I'll just figure it out myself" / range grinding / tips from buddies — fails because it lacks personalized diagnosis, accountability, and emotional/mental coaching.

**Where all competitors fall short:** Nobody in the area combines world-class coaching methodology, experiential social programming, and genuine care for the whole person — not just their swing.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Coaching philosophy:** Guides and coaches rather than mandates. "I don't tell you what to do — I help you discover what works for your body and your game."
- **World-class training lineage:** Has trained under and hosted Karl Morris, Fred Shoemaker, James Sieckmann, Pia Nilsson & Lynn Marriott (Vision54), Dr. Joseph Parent (Zen Golf), and Gareth Raflewski (tour coach) at Mad River.
- **Whole-person approach:** Addresses the mental/emotional game (self-talk, emotional resilience, cortisol/fight-or-flight) alongside the technical — not just swing mechanics.
- **Thursday Night Experiences:** Wine & Wedges, Scotch & Short Game, 9 & Dine, Links Night — nobody else offers golf-as-lifestyle events.
- **Mad River Ladies Golf Academy:** Dedicated programming for women seeking community + improvement.
- **Technology in service of the student:** TrackMan and OnForm used to understand the student's swing — not as a sales gimmick.
- **20+ years of experience** as a CPGA Class A Professional.
**How we do it differently:** Ryan treats every lesson as a relationship, not a transaction. The student trusted him enough to ask for help, and he takes that seriously. The goal is always clarity — cutting through the noise of tips, YouTube, and self-doubt to give the student one truth about their game.
**Why that's better:** Students leave with understanding, not confusion. They get a clear path forward instead of more "tips to try."
**Why customers choose Ryan:** "I hear you're the guy." The experience is different — they feel coached, not lectured. The warmth, intelligence, and genuine care come through immediately.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Lessons are expensive" | One clear lesson saves you from buying 3 new drivers and watching 200 YouTube videos. A 15-lesson series brings the hourly rate down to ~CA$117. Investing in yourself is the best investment in your game. |
| "I'm not good enough for lessons" | You're exactly who lessons are for. Beginners are welcome — the Ladies Golf Series and clinics are designed specifically as comfortable entry points. |
| "I can figure it out on my own" | How's that going? YouTube doesn't know your body. Your buddy's tips work for their swing, not yours. If grinding on the range was going to work, it would have worked by now. |
| "I don't know what to expect" | That's normal. The first lesson is a conversation — we figure out your goals, look at your swing, and give you something real to work with. No pressure, no jargon, no judgement. |

**Anti-persona:** Someone looking for a quick-fix magic tip. Someone who wants to be told exactly what to do and doesn't want to engage in the process. Someone who only values distance and doesn't care about the experience of playing.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push (away from current state):** Confused by contradictory advice, frustrated by inconsistency, embarrassed on the course, tired of wasting money on equipment and tips that don't work, negative self-talk ruining their enjoyment of the game.
**Pull (toward RR Golf):** "I hear you're the guy," wanting a different kind of learning experience, wanting a coach who cares — not just someone who takes their money and gives them drills, the appeal of experiential programming (Thursday Nights, Ladies Academy).
**Habit (what keeps them stuck):** YouTube is free and easy, range grinding feels productive, "I'll figure it out eventually," reluctance to spend money on lessons.
**Anxiety (about switching):** Intimidation — "Will he judge my swing?" Not knowing what to expect. Feeling uncoordinated or "not good enough." The vulnerability of asking for help.

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| TrackMan | Launch monitor technology that measures ball flight and club data — used to understand (not dictate) the swing |

| On-Course Lesson | Playing lesson on the actual golf course, not the range — where the "sabertooths" live |
