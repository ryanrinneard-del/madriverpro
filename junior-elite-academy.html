Schedule

How the Season Runs

The season is split into two phases that match how serious junior players actually live their year. Saturdays during the school term, then Tuesdays and Fridays once school is out and tournament season is in full swing.

Phase One · School Term

Saturdays at 9:30 AM · 2 hours of coaching

Dates · May 23, May 30, Jun 6, Jun 13, Jun 20, Jun 27 (six Saturdays)  ·  Format · 90 min coaching block + 30 min targeted skill work

The boys book their own tee times after the session — weekend tee times at Mad River are a member-lottery system, so lining the group up at one set time isn't realistic. Each player gets a specific on-course assignment and tracks the round in the portal once they're done.

Phase Two · Summer

Tuesdays 10:00–11:30 AM · Fridays 11:00–12:30 PM · 90 min each

Dates · Jul 7 through Sep 4 (one-week Canada Day gap; no Fri Aug 7 — CJGA Polair National Championship week)  ·  Format · 90 min Tuesday + 90 min Friday + on-course assignment

School is out, tournaments are running, the work compresses. Two sessions a week split between range coaching and short-game / putting blocks. Players complete a self-guided 9 or 18 holes the same day and submit the round in the portal.

Attendance. Fifteen weeks total — five more than last year. Players are not expected to attend every session. Tournaments, school commitments, family travel, and life will mean missing some. Aim for ten weeks of the fifteen. Each session is self-contained, so missing a week doesn't break the arc.

Tournament Pathway

Simcoe County Junior Golf Tour — 2026

The SCJGT, directed by Terry O'Brien and run by PGA of Canada professionals, is the most accessible competitive pathway for junior golfers in the region. SCJGT events award CJGA order-of-merit points and qualify the boys' and girls' overall season champions into the CJGA Polair National Championship at Tangle Creek (Aug 7–10). Junior Elite players arrive at every event with a specific game plan built around that week's session work.

Important: Unlike last year's Pepsi Team Tour, the SCJGT is not a team event. Parents register their junior online individually for each event they wish to play. Tour entry fees are separate from and not included in the summer program tuition.

Tools & Technology

What We Teach With

TrackMan

Ball Flight & Launch

Every full-swing session tracks club path, face angle, strike location, launch, spin, and carry. No guessing.

TPI Level 3

Physical Screen + App

Full Week 1 movement screen is mandatory. Every player installs the TPI app and commits to the assigned mobility / stability program between sessions.

Stack System

Speed Training

The Stack Golf System drives all deliberate speed work. Progressive protocols, app-tracked, logged alongside the scorecard each week.

Vision54

Mental & Emotional

The Human Skills framework drives the routine, commitment, and composure work through Phase 2.

DECADE

Course Management

Scott Fawcett's strokes-gained-based strategy system is the backbone of the Tiger 5 work and all on-course decision training.

The Round Itself

The On-Course Arm of the Program

Each session is 90 minutes of coaching followed by the real work: playing the golf course. Players leave each session with a specific on-course assignment — a focus, a constraint, a stat to chase. The coach is not on the course with them; the structure is. This is how tournament skill is built.

The portal is the tracking system. No paper sheet needed — rrgolfperformance.com has the full hole-by-hole scorecard pre-loaded with Mad River yardages, tees, ratings, and the 5 Errors Framework (aka The Tiger 5).

After Every Round

Round Submission

Every round during the program goes into rrgolfperformance.com. The Junior Elite scorecard captures the data Ryan needs to coach off, and the auto-rollups mean players don't have to compute anything.

01

Pick the Course & Tees

Mad River scorecard for home rounds (all 7 tees, ratings, slope pre-loaded). Away scorecard for any other course — just enter rating + slope.

02

Hole-by-Hole Entry

Per hole the player logs: score & putts, tee direction (F/L/R/X), 1st putt distance, chip difficulty (Esy/Hrd), commit (Y/N), wedge approach (yes/no), penalty strokes.

03

Submit & Auto-Roll-Up

Computed automatically: FIR, GIR, total putts, three-putts, scrambling, fairway pattern, commitment %, and the 5 Errors Total. Auto-saves as you type. Front 9, Back 9, or 18.
