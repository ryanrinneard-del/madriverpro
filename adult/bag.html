Every club, every yardage. Carry is how far it flies, Total is where it stops. Customize the list — your bag isn't a template. Not everyone carries a 3-wood or a 4-iron, and yours might have a 4-hybrid, a 6-hybrid, or a gapped-out wedge setup. Make it yours.

Tip. Hit 8–10 balls with each club on a calm day. Drop your two worst and two best, then take the average of the middle. That's your number. Update it every month — yardages change with tempo, turf, and the season.

How we compute this. Wind affects every club differently because each club has its own trajectory — a driver runs flat and penetrates wind; a sand wedge floats and gets shoved around. We categorize your bag into low-flight (driver, woods, long irons), mid-flight (6–8 iron), and high-flight (9 iron through wedges), then scale the effect by your actual carry distance. Your personal carry : total ratio nudges the estimate up or down — a shot that rolls out a lot flies lower and is less wind-affected, and vice versa.
Disclaimer. Numbers assume a pure strike off a clean lie. Mishits, slopes, elevation, temperature, and altitude all move the number. Use these as a starting point, not a final answer — and as always, commit to the shot before you swing.
