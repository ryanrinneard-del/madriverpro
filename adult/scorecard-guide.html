Pencil-and-Paper Scorecard Guide

How to track your round — without your phone.

The Mad River scorecard has four blank rows below each hole. Here's how to use them to capture seven stats per hole with a pencil, then transcribe into the portal after the round. Designed so nothing gets in the way of actually playing.

Row 1The Result — Score & Tee Shot

Your standard scoring line, with one addition: a single letter next to the score showing where the drive went.

5
Your score on the hole. Write the big number.
F
Fairway hit. Use on par 4/5 only.
L
Missed left. Anywhere left of the fairway.
R
Missed right. Anywhere right of the fairway.
X
Penalty / lost ball. The unforced error. OB, water, or ball not found.

Row 2The Approach — GIR & Putting

Whether you reached the green, how far your first putt was, and how many putts you took. This row tells you if you're actually striking the ball or just scrambling for par.

G
Green in regulation. Reached the green in par−2 strokes. Leave blank if you missed.
25
First putt distance (feet). Your best guess. 5, 15, 40 — whatever's closest.
2
Total putts. Write it small, in the corner.

Row 3The Grind — Scrambling Context

Only use this row if you missed the green. Dash or leave blank if you hit the green. This is where we find out if your short game has a pulse.

S
Simple. Standard chip, plenty of green to work with, decent lie.
T
Tough. Short-sided, deep bunker, buried lie, tight lie over water. The one that asked for more than you had.
✓
Up and down. Saved par or better from off the green.
X
Failed. Bogey or worse after missing the green.

Row 4The Mental Audit — The Intent Grade

The most important row on the card — and the hardest to be honest about. Be brutally honest. You can hit a terrible shot and still mark C. You can hit a great shot and still mark W. This row tracks your process, not your result.

C
Committed. Picked a target, visualized the shot, swung without hesitation. Total conviction.
W
Weak. You doubted the club, peeked, steered it, got lazy, or second-guessed yourself mid-swing.
Why this matters. Over 18 holes, tracking commitment reveals a pattern. Maybe you're committed on tee shots but weak on chips. Maybe the back nine shows weak intent creeping in as you tire. Maybe pressure situations pull "W" out of you. The diagnosis is in the pattern — and the portal will graph your commitment percentage over time.

ExampleA par-4 bogey, broken down

Here's what a single hole looks like when you're tracking all four rows. Par 4, ended up with a bogey.

5 (R)

Row 3

Row 4

Translation: bogey (5 on a par 4), tee shot missed right. No GIR (blank), first putt was 12 feet, two putts total. Missed green was a simple chip (S) but you failed to get up and down (X). And the mental audit was honest — you knew the chip was rushed, weak intent (W). A bogey with a story.

The full workflow

  1. On the course: pencil and paper only. Mark each of the four rows for every hole. Takes ten seconds per hole. No phone.
  2. After the round: open the Mad River Scorecard in the portal, pick your tees, and transcribe the card hole-by-hole.
  3. The portal auto-derives FW%, GIR%, scrambling %, commitment %, bad-miss count, par-type averages, and more — all from the data you wrote on the paper card.
  4. Trends + the Path widget update automatically with the new round's data. You can see immediately where this round moved the needle (or didn't).
