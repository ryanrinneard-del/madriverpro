Elite Junior Development

A year-round competitive development program for elite junior golfers at Mad River Golf Club. Tournament preparation, weekly competition, and a clear pathway to college golf — coached by Ryan Rinneard, PGA of Canada.

Built for juniors who want more.

For competitive players aged 15–18 who are ready to take their game beyond lessons and into tournament golf — with the coaching, structure, and pathway to make it happen.

🏆

Get tournament-ready through regional and provincial tours. We identify the right events, handle registration logistics, and prepare players mentally and technically for competitive rounds.

Weekly Competition

Scholarship Pathway

A structured roadmap from junior golf to college recruiting. Tournament resume building, NCAA eligibility, academic planning, and guidance through every step of the scholarship process.

Mental Performance

Tournament golf is a different game. Pre-shot routines, pressure management, post-round reflection, and the mindset work that separates practice from performance under pressure.

At the Club

Mad River Junior Competitive Series

A season-long weekly competition designed for elite juniors who want to compete — without the pressure of a rigid schedule. Play when you can, miss when you need to. Points accumulate all season.

Every Wednesday evening, 9 holes, rotating formats. The season runs May through September (~20 weeks), culminating in the Mad River Junior Championship — 18 holes, stroke play, no mulligans.

1

Stroke Play

Individual scoring — gross and net. The core format. Real scores, real pressure, official handicap rounds.

2

Match Play

Random-draw head-to-head pairings. Different mindset, different strategy — learn to compete hole by hole.

3

Best Ball Teams

Random 2-person teams. Builds camaraderie, takes individual pressure off, and teaches team golf.

4

Skills Challenges

Short game nights — up-and-down contests, closest to the pin, putting competitions. Pure scoring zone work.

The Goal

The pathway to college golf.

For juniors and parents who want to understand how competitive junior golf in Ontario leads to college scholarship opportunities in Canada and the United States.

1
Ages 14–15 · Foundation

Build Your Competitive Resume

Compete consistently on local and regional tours — SCJGT, Pepsi-Titleist, and MJT events. Establish a Golf Canada handicap and begin building your Junior Golf Pathway ranking. Focus on posting consistent scores rather than chasing low rounds.

2
Ages 15–16 · Visibility

Expand to Provincial & National Events

Add CJGA events and Golf Ontario Championships to your schedule. Target the Next Wave high-performance program (U15/U17 selection based on Pathway rankings). Begin registering with the NCAA Eligibility Center and take the SAT/ACT.

3
Ages 16–17 · Recruiting Window

Active College Recruiting

This is when D1 programs make verbal offers and D2/NAIA coaches are actively watching. Build a golf resume (tournament results, handicap, video). Use the MJT College Recruitment Program. Begin reaching out to coaches directly — email, video, and stats.

4
Ages 17–18 · Commitment

Official Visits & Commitment

Narrow your list, take official visits, and make your commitment. D3 and NAIA windows stay open longer — real opportunities exist beyond D1. Ensure all NCAA academic eligibility requirements are complete. Sign your letter of intent.

Ryan's Role in the Process

I've been coaching these kids for years — I know their games, their strengths, and where they need to grow. My job is to help them navigate the tournament landscape, build a competitive resume that coaches notice, and prepare them technically and mentally for the next level. I also work directly with parents to make sure the academic side stays on track. This isn't just about golf — it's about giving these young people every opportunity they've earned.
