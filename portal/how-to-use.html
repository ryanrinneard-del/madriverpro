01 · HOME BASE

The Dashboard

Every time you open the portal, you land here. It's designed to answer "what do I do today?" in the first three seconds.

What you'll see

  • Today's Plan — the navy card at the top. Up to three action items picked from your real data (a round without a reflection, pre-work from your last lesson, "book your next lesson," etc.). Tap any item, it takes you straight to the thing to do.
  • Your Game Snapshot — three plain-English metrics Ryan has set for you ("Driver: Needs Work", "Wedges: Improving"). Updated after lessons.
  • Last Coach Input — a one-sentence pull from Ryan's most recent lesson debrief. Fast reminder of what to keep in mind.
  • This Week — the current week card from the 15-week Weekly Playbook with your focus and the week's deliverables. Click "Open week" to see the full breakdown.
  • Quick Log — three big buttons (Round / Tiger 5 / History). Get you to the most-used actions in one tap.
Daily habit: open the portal once in the morning, check Today's Plan. That's it. If there's something to do, it'll be right there.

02 · LOG A ROUND

Logging a round — 3 taps, done

The form is designed so you can log a round on your phone in the parking lot after you finish. Only Score is required. Everything else is optional.

The essentials (top of the form)

  • Date (auto-fills to today)
  • Course (optional)
  • Score (required)
  • Holes (defaults to 18)

Optional — as much or as little as you want

  • Fairways / Greens / Putts
  • Tiger 5 tally (auto-fills if you used the live tracker on course)
  • Reflection (What went well? What needs work?)
  • Advanced (tees, format, 3-putts, penalties, routine score, one thing to work on)
Ryan reviews every submission. Even if you only have time to punch in a score, do it. The pattern over the season tells him more than any single round.

03 · ON-COURSE TRACKER

Tiger 5 — the five avoidable errors

Tiger Woods famously tracked five specific errors in every round and tried to keep his total under 6 across an entire tournament. This tool does the same for you.

The five errors

  1. Bogey or worse on a par 5
  2. Double bogey or worse (blow-up hole)
  3. Blown easy save (on or near green in a good lie, didn't get up-and-down)
  4. Three-putt
  5. Bogey with a scoring club (9-iron or shorter)

Two ways to use it

Live tracking — open the Tiger 5 page on your phone before the round. Tap the + button on each error as it happens. Your tally auto-saves on your device, so you can close the tab between holes. When you finish, tap "Log this round →" and your Tiger 5 counts flow straight into the round submission.

Paper card — print the 6×4" scorecard from the Tiger 5 page (fits a regular scorecard holder). Tick the boxes on each hole. After the round, type your totals into Submit Round.

Working target: 1–2 Tiger 5 events per round. Tiger's benchmark was ≤ 6 across four tournament rounds.

05 · SCORING TOOL

The Wedge Matrix

One page, four wedges, four swing lengths (Full / ¾ / ½ / Knockdown). 16 calibrated carry numbers. Built once on the range, referenced forever.

How to build it

  1. On the range, hit 5 balls with each wedge at each swing length.
  2. Record the average carry, not the best.
  3. Type the numbers into the matrix. Autosaves to your portal.

The wind calculator

Below the main matrix, a second live table. Pick direction (Head / None / Tail), slide the wind speed, and the whole table recalculates with today's wind factored in — color-coded red (fall short) or green (fly long).

Below that, a write-up on why tour pros launch wedges under 30° and how to build the knocked-down wedge that cuts through wind.

Print it: the matrix prints to a 6×4" card that fits a scorecard holder. Pocket it. Reference it mid-round.

06 · KNOW YOUR CLUBS

Map My Bag

Every club in your bag with its carry and total distance. Essential for course management — you can't commit to a shot if you don't know your numbers.

Use a launch monitor (TrackMan, GCQuad, or even a portable like Rapsodo) to get real carry numbers. In a pinch, hit 10 balls with each club on a flat range with flags at known distances and estimate.

07 · COACHING JOURNAL

Lessons — Every Session, Logged

After every lesson with Ryan, he logs a debrief in your portal: what you worked on, the one key takeaway, and pre-work for the next session. It shows up in two places:

  • Last Coach Input on your dashboard (the 1-sentence summary)
  • Every Session, Logged on your dashboard — the full history

Tap any lesson row to see the full detail: focus, debrief, pre-work, and a place for you to add your own feel-check reflection.

Come to lessons ready: glance at the last debrief on your way to the range. Arrive knowing what you're working on. The lesson becomes a tune-up, not a re-explanation.
