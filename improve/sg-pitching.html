Pitching.

The partial-wedge distance matrix — three reliable numbers per wedge, built by pattern, not by feel.
Primary source: James Sieckmann · supporting: Dave Pelz

Pitching is the skill that separates good amateurs from good players. It's next up after chipping. If your 40-80 yard shots are what's killing your scores, ping me and I'll prioritize this one.

Planned outline

  • Section 01 — Why "feel" doesn't work for partial wedges. The cognitive load of trying to feel a 40-yard shot vs. executing a known distance pattern. Sieckmann's argument for building a matrix.
  • Section 02 — The Wedge Matrix. How to build 3 reliable yardages per wedge — full, three-quarter, half — across PW/GW/SW/LW. 12 distances you actually own.
  • Section 03 — Sieckmann's face-first strike pattern. The technique that makes partial wedges predictable — how the face stays open through impact, why the bounce is your friend.
  • Section 04 — Pelz's arm-clock system. The older approach — back-to-the-clock positions (9 o'clock, 10 o'clock, 11 o'clock) as the way to calibrate distance. Where it still works.
  • Section 05 — Practice structure for wedge matrix. How many reps per distance to actually "own" a number. Why practicing all four wedges in one session is less effective than one wedge per session.
