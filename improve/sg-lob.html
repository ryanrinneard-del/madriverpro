The Lob Shot.

The shot you should hit last, not first.
Primary source: Dave Pelz · supporting: James Sieckmann
Coming soon

This skill page is being written.

The lob is the short-game shot amateurs reach for most often and need least often. When a high-soft shot is genuinely the only answer — over a bunker to a tight pin, off a firm lie to an elevated green — this page is how to execute it without the fear that most amateurs carry.

Planned outline

  • Section 01 — When NOT to hit a lob. The shot selection conversation first, because 80% of the lobs amateurs attempt are wrong-shot decisions.
  • Section 02 — When a lob is the right answer. Three scenarios where no other shot works. Learning to spot them on the course.
  • Section 03 — The lie that allows a lob. What the ball sits on matters more than what you're hitting over. Fluffy lies vs. tight lies; why a 60° wedge off hardpan is the mistake.
  • Section 04 — The technique. Open face, open stance, full acceleration, bounce glides through the grass. Pelz's explanation of why the clubhead speed has to be high even though the ball travels short.
  • Section 05 — How to practice without losing confidence. The trap of "working on the lob shot" at the range and what to do instead.
