Planned outline

  • Section 01 — Why chipping beats lobbing. The default short-game shot. Lower-risk trajectory, wider margin for error, bigger target.
  • Section 02 — The one-club, one-swing approach. Sieckmann's pattern: a single motion, changing only the club to change trajectory. Less to think about under pressure.
  • Section 03 — Landing-spot thinking. Pick a landing spot on the green, let the ball release to the hole. The physics of first-bounce + run-out with each club.
  • Section 04 — Lie reading. What tight lies, fluffy lies, and collar lies tell you about club selection and technique.
  • Section 05 — Practice structure. How to build chipping reps that transfer to the course instead of feeling good on the practice green only.
